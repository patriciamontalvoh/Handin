Responsive Redesign

Patricia Montalvo Hernandez

Introduction

The goal of this assignment was to redesign a page of an interface to provide an enhanced accessibility, visual design, responsiveness, and usability. To do so, different webpages were analyzed, and an ineffective webpage was selected for its redesign. Once the webpage had been selected, the complete design process was executed: from brainstorming to the development of different high-fidelity mock-ups. Finally, the selected page was coded in a responsive manner to be suitable and adaptable to any device and their layouts.

The chosen website was the Home page of the Rhode Island department of health. This website provides information and resources related to public health in the state of Rhode Island. It includes sections like diseases, health and wellness, or vital health records. The webpage also offers updates on health news, topics of interest, and resources for healthcare professionals and the general public. It also features links to health services, insurance information, and community-oriented details.

Despite the need for a simple and accessible design, this webpage lacks organization and does not have a practical layout to ensure the users can navigate it efficiently. Given the many problems identified and the low evaluation scores for accessibility and overall UI, I choose tho redesign this webpage in order to modify its design to a more straightforward and user-friendly interface.

Usability problems:

• Excessive information with a lack of order and hierarchy

• Lack of order and composition.

• Lack of tags for accessibility

• Too many colors, icons, and different shapes.

• Difficulty finding useful buttons, such as the language tool.

• Many items are shown in the screen, but the information cannot be absorbed intuitively.

• Lack of contrast in items such as the menu.
